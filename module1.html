Overview

This module introduces the concept of strategic leadership in the sustainability era. Learners will examine why sustainability is now a central leadership priority, apply systems-thinking principles, and evaluate real-world frameworks that drive sustainable transformation.

Session 1 – The Sustainability Imperative

  • Objectives: Explain why sustainability is a leadership priority; describe systems-thinking principles; identify sustainable-leadership characteristics.
  • Key Readings: Waddock & McIntosh (2021); Senge et al., The Necessary Revolution.

Topics: Climate crisis, resource depletion, and social inequality; regulatory / investor pressures; stakeholder expectations for ethics and transparency.

Session 2 – Systems Thinking for Sustainability

Systems thinking is a holistic approach to understanding interconnections, feedback loops, and leverage points that influence organizational outcomes.

  • Organizations as living systems – dynamic, adaptive, learning.
  • Interconnections – viewing relationships beyond silos.
  • Leverage points – small shifts create large, lasting change.

Senge’s Principles: Leverage Points, Shared Vision, Mental Models — driving resilient and adaptive organizations.

Session 3 – Characteristics of Sustainable Leaders

  • Purpose-driven mindset – mission beyond profit.
  • Stakeholder orientation – inclusive and collaborative.
  • Long-term strategic perspective – future-focused decisions.
  • Resilience & adaptability – embracing innovation and learning from failure.

Session 4 – Leadership Frameworks for Sustainability

Analyzes models guiding modern sustainable leadership:

  • Sustainable Leadership Model: Knowledge of systems; cultivating sustainable practices; building communities of practice.
  • ESG-Driven Leadership: Integrates Environmental, Social & Governance principles into decisions.
  • Systems-Thinking Leadership: Maps relationships and leverage points for change.
  • Relational & Collective Leadership: Leadership as a shared process for common purpose.

Case Applications: Tesla (eco-leadership), DSM Firmenich (culture integration), Brazilian organizations (shared vision and systems thinking).

Session 5 – Visionary Thinking & Organizational Culture

Explores how a shared vision and aligned culture enable sustainable transformation.

  • Vision sets direction and inspires collective action.
  • Culture embeds vision into daily practice through shared values and psychological safety.
  • Examples: Tesla’s mission for sustainable energy; Unilever’s Sustainable Living Plan.

Session 6 – Sustainable Business Models

Explains how business models can align profit with positive social and environmental impact.

  • Circular Economy Models: Reuse / recycle resources (e.g., Tesla, Interface).
  • Base-of-the-Pyramid Models: Affordable solutions for low-income markets (e.g., Unilever Shakti).
  • Shared Value Models: Profit through social impact (e.g., Ben & Jerry’s, PepsiCo).

Session 7 – Drivers & Barriers to Sustainable Leadership

Drivers: Regulatory incentives, consumer demand, technology, leadership values.

Barriers: Cultural resistance, short-termism, knowledge gaps, stakeholder complexity.

Session 8 – Innovation & Technology for Sustainability

Highlights technological innovation as a driver of sustainable entrepreneurship.

  • Renewables, EVs, green buildings, AI & IoT for sustainability.
  • Concepts from Kern & Hoppmann (2018) and Rockström (Planetary Boundaries).
  • Emphasis on ecosystem support and policy integration.

Session 9 – Stakeholder Engagement & Regulatory Trends

  • Stakeholder mapping and collaboration for SDG alignment.
  • Regulatory focus in India: CSR mandates, BRSR, green finance, EPR rules, SEBI ESG frameworks.
  • Examples: Unilever, Patagonia, Tesla – stakeholder engagement and policy alignment.

Summary: Sustainable leadership demands systems thinking, visionary mindsets, inclusive stakeholder engagement, and technological innovation to achieve transformational change for people, planet, and profit.
